COVID-19 Symptoms

Know the signs and when to seek medical attention

Fever

High temperature (above 38°C or 100.4°F) is one of the most common symptoms.

Dry Cough

Persistent dry cough that's new for you (not due to another known condition).

Fatigue

Unusual tiredness or exhaustion that isn't related to physical exertion.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing or feeling breathless even when resting.

Loss of Taste/Smell

New loss of taste or smell without any nasal congestion.

Body Aches

Muscle or body aches that aren't related to physical activity.

Emergency Warning Signs: Trouble breathing, persistent pain or pressure in the chest, new confusion, inability to wake or stay awake, bluish lips or face. Seek medical attention immediately.

Prevention Measures

How to protect yourself and others from COVID-19

Wear a Mask

Wear a mask in public settings, especially when maintaining social distancing is difficult.

  • Covers nose and mouth
  • Fits snugly against sides of face
  • Wash reusable masks regularly

Wash Your Hands

Clean your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.

  • Before eating or preparing food
  • After being in a public place
  • After touching surfaces in public areas

Maintain Distance

Keep at least 6 feet (about 2 arms' length) from other people.

  • Avoid crowded places
  • Limit physical contact
  • Stay home when possible

Get Vaccinated

Authorized COVID-19 vaccines help protect you from getting sick.

  • Follow local vaccination guidelines
  • Get booster shots when eligible
  • Continue precautions after vaccination

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about COVID-19

COVID-19 is the infectious disease caused by the most recently discovered coronavirus. This new virus and disease were unknown before the outbreak began in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. COVID-19 is now a pandemic affecting many countries globally.

The virus is transmitted through direct contact with respiratory droplets of an infected person (generated through coughing and sneezing), and touching surfaces contaminated with the virus. The virus may survive on surfaces for several hours, but simple disinfectants can kill it.

While we are still learning about how COVID-19 affects people, older persons and persons with pre-existing medical conditions (such as high blood pressure, heart disease, lung disease, cancer or diabetes) appear to develop serious illness more often than others.

Antibiotics should not be used as a means of prevention or treatment of COVID-19. They should only be used as directed by a physician to treat a bacterial infection.

COVID-19 vaccines are highly effective at preventing severe illness, hospitalizations, and death. The effectiveness can vary depending on the specific vaccine and variants circulating. Vaccines also reduce the risk of spreading the virus that causes COVID-19. Booster shots help maintain protection as immunity may decrease over time.
